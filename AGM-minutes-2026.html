  11. Club development plan and ClubSports365 system

    12. Ben Phillips provided a brief overview of the ClubSports365 system and the advantages it offers over Pitchero in terms of being a single place to manage the club's playing and off-field operations. He also explained the following additions to the club development plan:

    1. Improved governance: acquiring the top tier of the revamped Clubmark accreditation, improved budgeting and a review of the club's status

    Nick Swinburne stated that the club has grown exponentially and the infrastructure of the club needs to evolve to support it. He re-iterated that the club development plan is an evolving document and forms part of a wider review of the club's strategy that the executive committee will undertake in 2026.

  13. Changes to the constitution and remit/governance policy

    14. The executive committee proposed the following changes:

    1. Constitution (quorum): Replace two of chair, secretary, treasurer with two of chair, president, director of cricket and operations chair and one of secretary or treasurer or their deputies.

      Explanation: Under the current wording, a general meeting could be quorate but have no recognisable chair

    2. Constitution (membership): In membership add, a) Having an active membership in ClubSports365 shall be the sole determinant of an individual’s playing or social membership of the club, b) The committees shall ensure that payment in instalments is an option and c) The club membership year covers 1st April to 31st March.

      Explanation: This is to establish that the club is adopting ClubSports365 as the indicator of membership and to make other necessary changes and clarifications.

    3. Constitution and Remit/Governance: To rename club captain to director of cricket.

      Explanation: This is to be consistent with ECB defined roles.

    The changes were proposed en-bloc by Peter Mundy and seconded by Tina Foley

    Resolved: Carried unanimously (one abstention)
