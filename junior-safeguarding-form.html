Winsley CC recognises the need to ensure the safeguarding of all children. As part of this commitment, we will not permit photographs, video images or other images of children to be taken or used without the consent of the parent/guardian. Winsley CC will take all reasonable steps to ensure that these images are used solely for the purpose they are intended, which is the promotion and celebration of cricketing activities.

Winsley CC follows the guidance issued in ’Safe Hands - Welfare of Young People in Cricket’ which is available at http://www.ecb.co.uk/safehands. If photographs or video e.g. team or action shots are published on our website or on our social media pages individuals will not ordinarily be identified. Where a child or young person is identified it will only be with the explicit consent of parents. Please note: Winsley CC understands that there are circumstances under which a parent would not wish their child to be photographed. Whilst Winsley CC will do all that it can to ensure the safety of children during photographed events, it is the responsibility of the parent concerned to ensure that if their child is not to be photographed and partakes in an activity and is by the nature of the activity inadvertently photographed or filmed, Winsley CC cannot be held responsible.

Please note that where consent is withheld, the club will make reasonable efforts to manage this (e.g., positioning the child outside of filming zones), but complete exclusion from incidental capture cannot be guaranteed during open, public or group activities.

Social Media Policy

Social media is an important aspect of promoting Winsley CC and it’s activities. When publishing comments and pictures about Winsley CC, members must always be conscious that they are representatives of the Club. Please ensure all posts support the Club’s views and ethos. 

Social media posts that could damage the reputation of the Club will be reviewed by the Clubs Committee and could result in some form of disciplinary action (e.g. Warning, suspension etc.) being taken against the member concerned.

I understand that this includes private or closed-group social media activity where posts may still reasonably be associated with the Club.
